The Challenge

Data that is descriptive,
not actionable

While ICDS generates extensive data across nutrition, health and service delivery, decision-making at various administrative levels is constrained by systemic gaps. The data exists — but remains locked in formats that do not drive action.

Frontline supervisors and administrators require decision-support tools, not just reports, to enable timely interventions, targeted monitoring and efficient resource deployment.

⚡

Fragmented Data Sources

Nutrition, health, and service delivery data siloed across disconnected systems with no unified view.

👁

Lack of Real-Time Visibility

Delayed reporting cycles prevent timely response to emerging risks at AWC and Block levels.

🎯

Absence of Prioritization Logic

No automated mechanisms to surface high-risk cases or underperforming centres for targeted action.
